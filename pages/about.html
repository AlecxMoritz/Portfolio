My name is Alecx Moritz. I graduated in 2012 from Westfield High School as a twelve-year senior. My major areas of study were art, music, and history. I began my fascination with coding when I was about twelve, and I started to build maps for a game I played called Furcadia. Years passed and the obsession settled into a hobby, but I was never quite satisfied with where I was heading. After highschool I went into the workforce as a delivery driver for Domino's Pizza, and briefly was a student at Ivy Tech. I eventually left Domino's after my manager training was put on indefinite pause. I then began working as a cook on the St. Vincent West hospital campus.

I then settled into a job as a delivery driver in fall of 2014 at Jimmy John's, and six months later I became an assistant manager, and later a general manager. I learned quite a bit during my time with JJ's, and would say I am quite proud of my accomplisments there; however, while I enjoyed this position, it was not my passion, and left me trying to pursue technology and coding as a hobby.

I had always known traditional school environments were not for me. Luckily my mom had found out about the Eleven-Fifty Academy, and told me I should look into it. Also luckily, I did. I made an interview appointment, and less than half-way through my interview, I knew I was in the right place, and it would be just a question of getting all my finances straight so I could attend.

I am currently learning JavaScript, HTML, and CSS, and hope to pick up many more languages by the time I graduate in May. After graduation I hope to continue my learning whith more about game development and security.
