March 26th, 2018

Gold Badge Earned

This last week at EFA has been wild. The first part was mostly work time, were my cohort-mates and I worked hard on our API projects, portfolios, and any changes we wanted to have implemented for Thursday's presentation.

My projects for the week were finishing up the Ghost's JS file, which gave me more trouble than I had originally thought it would. I tried using jQuery to levitate him at first, but ended up using webkit-animations. The result was exactly what I was hoping for. The ghost rises and falls and his shadow will grow and shrink along with him. I also needed to rework MechaHut so that all of my images would render after it had been deployed. My last project to finish was the portfolio itself. I implemented a carousel, that at first worked perfectly fine, but upon deployment, I lost an image! It was incredibly nervewracking to try to figure out why it would display locally but not once I had pushed it to github. The cards eventually fell in my favor and I was about to get it sorted out Wednesday night, and redeployed without issue.

After presenting on Thursday morning, I felt inredilbe amounts of pride in both myself, and my cohort as a whole. It had only been three weeks since we've started, but we all had such immpressive, and very unique projects to display. That feeling was cemented on Friday during a trip to Pinhead's.

We recieved out Gold Badges during a bowling outting. It was greate break from the heads-down coding we'd been doing lately, and felt great after all that pre-presentation stress had been lifted off my shoulders. It was great to get to go out with everyone and relax and just focus on the cumulation of all the work we've been doing. My cohort has worked incredibly hard and made extremely impressive strides.

Our next challenge is React, and I'm sure we'll do just as well!

March 19th, 2018

Hello again, it's still Alecx. This last week's theme was APIs, and a multitude of API applets, and we were assigned to create out own API applet to present with our Gold Badge on this coming Thursday. We've also been learning about DOM manipulation, and how to use that with our API applets.

The class has been hard at work on our many projects, and I am proud to say that my static webstore, and portfolio are finished. My ghost still needs a bit of javascript, and there are some more styling elements I would like to add to my Move Dex, however the logic, and the bare-bones of my web applet are finished!

The rest of this week is mostly a work week, so my classmates and I will be spending most of our time finishing up our projects, with some time for shorter lessons. My personal projects for this week include the ghost's JavaScript, a canvas on this portfolio for an update I have planned in a little while, and if I have enough time, redesigning Mecha Hut's look.
